Neural computers go beyond video--based world models~\citep{ha2018world}: they aim for an executable neural universe in which modeling, runtime-state evolution, and environment interaction are unified within a single learned neural substrate.
%
Human inputs—keystrokes, mouse gestures, or natural-language commands—flow through a bidirectional I/O bus into the model.
%
For each model, computation unfolds entirely within the latent space that also produces a near-realistic interactive experience.
%
The updated state then sculpts the next video frame, which also serves as implicit, writable working memory.
%
As an existence proof, we present two video-based neural computers: \nccligen{} for terminal workflows and \ncguiworld{} for desktop interaction, backed by detailed experiments and analyses. Together, these results suggest an early yet promising research direction toward learned systems that internalize computation, memory, and interface within a single latent runtime state.
